Slant Six

170 cid

The first Slant Six to roll off the assembly line in 1959 was 170 in3 (2.8L). It produced a modest 101 hp and 155 lb. ft. of torque; while it may not have been the beefiest powerplant for Dodge's heavy-duty lineup, it was supremely reliable and fuel-efficient.

198 cid

The latest of the three Slant-Six configurations, the 198 cid (3.2L) was introduced in 1970. Producing 125 hp and 180 lb. ft. of torque, it was a step above the 170 but still lacked the attractive power and torque characteristics of the 225 (and the V8s).

225 cid

Officially, the 225 produced 145 hp @ 4000 rpm and 215 lb. ft. of torque @ 2800. When GM ran a competitive analysis of the engine in March 1961, they found that it actually reached a peak torque of 190 lb. ft. @ 1400 rpm (as installed in the car). The Slant's impressive torque characteristics made it a great workhorse, even in Dodge's heavier trucks.

The Poly 318

The Poly 318 had a similar round combustion chamber to the Hemi, but was cheaper to produce. Manufactured from 1956-1966, the Poly 318 made more power than its successor (the LA-318), but was extremely heavy; it only weighed a few pounds less than Dodge's largest powerplant, the 440.

The LA 318

The L stands for "Light", since this engine weighed 50 lbs less than the Poly. The LA 318 produced 210 hp and 280 lb-ft of torque; plenty of power to haul your toys around. Released in 1967, the LA engine would only be found in later-model Swepties, but it stayed in the Dodge pickup family until 1991!
